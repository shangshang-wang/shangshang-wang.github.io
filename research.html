Research at ShanghaiTech

As a Master's candidate, my research is centered around the high-level theme of ‘‘Bandits for Network Intelligence’’ and my works delve into the integration of two inter-connected aspects:

1. (Algorithm-Oriented Component) The re-assessment of bandit learning methods, specifically considering practical operational constraints like budget and fairness.

2. (Application-Oriented Component) The implementation of these refined methods to bolster networked systems, including edge computing, federated learning, and crowdsourcing systems.

In other words, my current research mainly focuses on how to enhance real-world networked systems with bandit learning methods under practical constraints, namely, ‘‘Constrained Bandit Learning for Networked Systems’’.
