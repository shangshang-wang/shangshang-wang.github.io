Research

My current research as a Master's candidate primarily focuses on “AI for Networks” at a high level, which highlights the importance of incorporating

  • the practical engineering components into AI technology and

  • the application of AI tools to enhance networked systems.

To advance in this direction, my research approach can be categorized into two distinct styles:

  • Algorithm-oriented research, i.e., reevaluating machine learning tools, such as bandits and neural networks, with a focus on practical operational considerations.

  • Application-oriented research, i.e., utilizing machine learning-based optimization for real-world systems, such as edge computing, federated learning, and crowdsourcing systems, among others.
